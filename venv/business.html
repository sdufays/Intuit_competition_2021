How to Help Your Business


Diversity Sells.



The more diverse a community, the more creative it becomes. Whether it is through improving the bottom line of companies or leading to breakthrough innovations, diversity encourages the search for novel information and perspectives, leading to better decision making and problem solving. Even simply being exposed to diversity can change the way you think because people with different backgrounds bring new information, so interacting with different people forces group members to prepare better, to anticipate alternative viewpoints, and to expect that reaching consensus will take effort.


This creativity pays off. Companies with diverse teams have a 20% higher rate of innovation and 19% higher innovation revenues, are 35% more likely to have returns above national industry medians, and 2.3 times the cash flow per employee.


In fact, beyond the obvious moral and ethical consequences, failing to elevate DEI—or enabling outright discrimination––will actually cause active harm to companies. The Center for American Progress estimates that workplace discrimination costs businesses $64 billion annually.


Aside from the innovation it brings, DEI also helps every employee show up each day without fear of being their true selves. This fosters higher degrees of engagement, productivity, and innovation that contribute to increased revenue.


Therefore, we’ve provided some toolkits, programs, and methods below that All-Inclusive recommends to incorporate within your business or organization to encourage innovation, increase customer satisfaction, and ensure that all employees and members are comfortable and heard.

  • Communication Toolkit -- to emphasize the importance of DEI to employees
  • DEI Progress Toolkit -- to measure the pay and demographic inequities present in a company and assess the progress it has made towards DEI.
  • Customer Toolkit -- to identity and broaden a company’s customer demographics so it caters towards the needs of their entire customer base.
  • Hiring Toolkit -- to mitigate implicit bias during the hiring process and to create objective methods to evaluate candidates.
  • We Hear You Toolkit -- to allow people to anonymously report microaggressions or give insight on how the community can be improved.
  • Getting in Contact With DEI Experts -- to give organizations the contact information of DEI experts to hire.
  • Unbiased Imagery -- to supply images with diverse people to each company to subconsciously promote DEI.
  • Gender Neutral Bathrooms -- to explain the need for and benefits of gender neutral bathrooms and how to incorporate them.
  • Language Resources to recommend tutoring/ESL services for companies to provide to employees who don’t have English as their first language.

Unbiased Imagery


Humans are visual creatures. We rely heavily on visual cues for basic adaptive behaviors, as well as more complex behaviors such as parental care and the formation of social hierarchies. So, when we search up an image of a doctor, entrepreneur, lawyer, or any other highly paying job, and all we see are cis white men, it creates a unconscious bias that tells us “Success is for cis white men; it’s not for me, and it’s not for people like me.”


When you think of a happy couple, what do you see? What about an excelling student, or a hardworking employee? When you picture a workplace, what do you see? People often picture white people as the default, and this is a very subtle unconscious bias that can be counteracted by greater exposure to images and media containing diversity. Seeing people from all different backgrounds working together opens people’s minds up to what is normal––because a conference room filled with only white men should not be the default. Diversity allows so many new perspectives to be expressed, and we should not accept or even settle for a lack of it. This article goes into depth about how we can use images to counteract unconscious bias and promote diversity, equity, and inclusion in all spaces (even online).
